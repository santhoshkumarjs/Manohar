Changing Trends in Education

Thoughts

Times have changed - are changing. As clichéd as it may sound, I am a witness to the change over every year, every month, every day. You may wonder what I am talking about. Being a Teacher / Professor / Lecturer, there is a lot that we learn as we go through the process of imparting knowledge.

I remember the time when I was a student, like it was yesterday. Yes, we’ve had our fair share of good teachers and bad teachers. We could NEVER ask a teacher a question during class. But there were some others who would want us to NOT stay quiet! We had to ask questions. We had some very interesting sessions and classes as well. all this without the intervention of technology.

But to this system there was one major positive or negative: We had to go to the library every time we had question. If the teacher was someone who was accessible, then yes, the teacher would sort out our doubts. But, if not, then we were left to the dire fate of having to go through a number of books to clear our doubts. And half the time, we were clueless as to whether the books we were going through were the right ones. It was a herculean task, so much so that we'd rather have a download of information from our friends or classmates before we rushed into the exam halls.

My times as a lecturer were slightly better. We would dabble with a little bit of power point presentations, have industry experts give students practical knowledge, and have students work on innovative projects.

​This was a phase of realisation for me. The amount of pressure a student went through was so much, very indescribable. ​There was always a lot of peer pressure, pressure to seem intelligent - which meant asking questions to the teacher would invariably result in the student or teacher judging you to be dumb or unintelligent. Not only was it discouraging to a student, but to a teacher as well. How else would we know if you, the student, understood the said subject well? If we taught you well? Your questions were our only form of feedback!

Fast forward to the present day. I see students these days are well informed of what they want to do and why they want to study a particular course. Students are up-to-date with the latest in the Industry or in the field of their study. They have their paths clear and they are more focused in what they want. And any information they need, is available on​ the net.

But I realise that sometimes they lose their direction and start second guessing themselves. And this is where we, as teachers, play a primary role. We now hold the responsibility to creating a learning environment that gives equal importance to practical application of knowledge that is being imparted. And this is where I believe that teaching and technology must go hand in hand. A classroom that can have the teacher explaining a concept with a visual presentation on how this concept works, for the student to fall back on, is nothing short of brilliance. And this is exactly why we appreciate our partnership with M-tutor. With M-Tutor, students have a better visual understanding of how a particular engineering concept works. Engineering essentials are simplified here, thanks to M-Tutor.

The best part about an online course is that you always have an option to fall back on! You don't understand a lesson, not to worry! M-Tutor gives you a visual description of the concept through their videos. I personally find M-Tutor's videos great and very descriptive in their explanation​. The importance they give to small concepts and their detailing is brilliant. What is even better and most important is the interaction and feedback here. You don’t understand a concept or want to understand it further? There is a platform to post your questions for which the responses are immediate! Don't like the way a particular concept is handled? Feedback - and the content is customized and changed to make you understand better.

Well, what I am trying to say here is, your job as a student is made easy and broken down for you. So why restrict yourself from learning more?

For all you students out there, every good teacher appreciates a great student who is willing to walk that extra mile to learn. So, what are you waiting for? What have you learnt today?

And to M-Tutor and team, I appreciate and commend your efforts in the field of education. And my sincere wishes to the team in their future endeavours and innovations in the field of education.

– Mr.Sunil Dhingra – Director – Kurukshetra University & Former Director UIET, Kurukshetra University.
