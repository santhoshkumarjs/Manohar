About

Ashoke Mullick born in Kolkata is a first class graduate from Government college of Arts and Craft. He was awarded the Cultural Scholarship by Department of Culture, Government of India during 1981-82. His paintings include a strong focus on the ordinary person – more specifically womankind and their contradictions that faced. The artist likes to portray the contemporary age where people have lost the human touch and have become increasingly impersonal in their behaviour; he touches the facts of life and reality. He canvases his beautiful thoughts on real and unreal world into a bold, vibrant imagination and energetic which is extremely appealing.

Group Exhibitions and Workshops

His works were featured in numerous group shows across the country and abroad, some of the noted shows include ‘Calling Bengal’, at Nitanjali Art Gallery, New Delhi; ‘Art of Drawing’ curated by Shuvaprasanna Bhattacharya at Gallery La Mere, Kolkata; ‘Evam’, presented by The Company Theatre at Tao Art Gallery, Mumbai; ‘Vote Tumikar’, CIMA Art Gallery, Kolkata; ‘Different strokes’, Polka Art Gallery, New Delhi; ‘Roots in Hue’, Jehangir Art Gallery, Mumbai; ‘India Surrealism’ curated by Prof. Sovam Som at Aakriti Art Gallery, Kolkata; ‘Digressing Domains’ curated by Sushma k Bahl presented at Nitanjal Art Gallery, New Delhi; ‘Unayan’, Nvya Art Gallery, New Delhi besides shows abroad in New York, France, Singapore, Dubai, Malaysia, Dhaka, Hong Kong, Japan, Germany, Stuttgart, and Sweden.

Mullick has participated in several art camps and workshops held at Max Muller Bhavan, Kolkata; Kalakriti Art Gallery, Hyderabad; 100 years of Cellular Jail, at Andaman & Nicobar Islands; Tata Steel, Jamshedpur; Khulana Art College, Khulana - Bangladesh; Koh-Samui, Thailand besides many more. Mullick’s works are a part of several Individual and Institutional collections in India and abroad.
